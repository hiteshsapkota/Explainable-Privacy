Explainable Privacy User Study Instructions

Suppose that you are a user of a photo-sharing website called ProShare. On ProShare, you also have several friends.

In the following pages, we will show you a series of photos. Suppose that these photos belong to you. We will provide a context for each photo, indicating whether you or your family members are in it or when you took that photo.

For each photo, you need to select one of the following options.

  • If you select the Share option, all your friends on Proshare will be able see the corresponding photo.
  • If you select the Do not share option, your photo will remain private—only you will be able to view your photo.
  • Select the Skip option if the context of the photo is not clear to you. Please do NOT use this option frequently.

Once you select one of the above options, click the Next button to go to the next photo. (If need be, you can click the Prev button to to revisit a previous photo and change your decision about it.)

  • You are required to select a sharing decision for a minimum 30 photos to receive the reward (USD 3) for the HIT.
  • After making sharing decision for 30 photos, you can make decisions for additional photos for a bonus reward. You will receive a bonus of five cents for each additional photo you label. You can get bonus for a maximum of 20 photos.
  • Skipped photos do not count for the minimum requirement or bonus.
  • The User Study Progress panel at the top of the page (like the one shown in this page) shows your progress.

After you make decisions for 30 photos, you can get the completion code from the Payment tab above.

If you have any questions about this study, please contact the researchers—Hitesh Sapkota (hxs1943@rit.edu) and Dr. Pradeep Murukannaiah (pkmvse@rit.edu). Please copy both researchers in your email.
